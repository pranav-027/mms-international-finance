## 1. Meaning

- International Finance = Study of **monetary interactions between countries**
- Focuses on:
  - Cross-border trade
  - Capital flows
  - Exchange rates
  - Financial markets

## 2. Importance

- Facilitates **globalization**
- Promotes **international trade and investment**
- Helps firms operate in **multiple countries**
- Enables efficient allocation of global capital

## 3. Key Areas

- Balance of Payments (BoP)
- Exchange Rates
- Foreign Exchange Market
- International Institutions (IMF, World Bank)
- Global Capital Markets

# UNIT 2: BALANCE OF PAYMENTS (BoP)

## 1. Definition

- BoP = **Systematic record of all economic transactions between a country and the rest of the world**

### IMF Definition

- Statistical statement summarizing transactions over a period

## 2. Features

- Based on **double-entry system**
- Always **balances**
- Measured over a period (year/quarter)
- Maintained in **foreign currency (usually USD)**

## 3. Double Entry Principle

- Every transaction has:
  - Credit (+) → inflow
  - Debit (-) → outflow

### Example

- Export:
  - Goods out → Credit
  - Foreign currency in → Credit entry

- Import:
  - Goods in → Debit
  - Payment out → Debit entry

---

## 4. Components of BoP

### A. Current Account

Includes:

#### 1. Trade in Goods (Visible)

- Exports and Imports

#### 2. Trade in Services (Invisible)

- IT services
- Banking
- Tourism

#### 3. Income (Primary Income)

- Interest
- Dividends
- Wages

#### 4. Transfers (Secondary Income)

- Remittances
- Gifts
- Foreign aid

### Important Concept:

- Temporary worker income → Income Account
- Permanent migrant remittance → Current Transfer

---

### B. Capital Account

Includes:

- Capital transfers
- Non-produced assets

#### Examples:

- Land abroad
- Patents
- Copyrights
- Natural resource rights

---

### C. Financial Account

Includes:

- Foreign Direct Investment (FDI)
- Portfolio Investment (FPI)
- Reserve Assets

---

## 5. BoP Accounting Example

(From table on page 11)

| ----------- | ------ | ----- |
| Exports     | +900   |       |
| Imports     |        | -1000 |
| Services    | +350   | -500  |
| Income      | +500   | -400  |
| Transfers   | +300   | -300  |

- Final BoP balances due to:
  - Capital flows
  - Errors & omissions

---

## 6. Current Account Deficit (CAD)

### Formula:

CAD = (Current Account Deficit / GDP) × 100

### Example:

- CAD = 100
- GDP = 3000

CAD % = 3.33%

### IMF Guideline:

- CAD should be **< 3% of GDP**

---

## 7. Role of RBI & IMF

- RBI:
  - Publishes BoP data
  - Manages forex reserves

- IMF:
  - Monitors global BoP
  - Advises countries
  - Ensures stability

---

## 8. Forex Reserves

- Used when:
  - Outflows > Inflows

- Increased when:
  - Inflows > Outflows

# UNIT 3: FOREIGN EXCHANGE MARKET (FOREX)

## 1. Meaning

- Global **OTC market for trading currencies**

## 2. Functions

- Currency conversion
- Determination of exchange rates
- Facilitates international trade

---

## 3. Features

- Decentralized
- 24-hour market
- High liquidity
- No physical location
- Highly transparent

---

## 4. Participants

- Central Banks
- Commercial Banks
- Corporations
- Governments
- Hedge Funds
- Retail Traders

---

## 5. Exchange Rate Determination

Factors:

- Demand & Supply
- Inflation
- Interest Rates
- Political Stability
- Market Speculation

---

## 6. Case: Rupee Depreciation (2025)

- ₹90/USD level crossed

### Causes:

- FPI outflows
- Strong USD
- Trade uncertainty
- High crude oil prices

### Impact:

- Higher imports cost
- Inflation increase
- Pressure on economy
